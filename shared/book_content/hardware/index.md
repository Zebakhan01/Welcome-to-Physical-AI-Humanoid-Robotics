# Hardware Guide: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Overview

This hardware guide provides comprehensive information about the components, systems, and platforms used in Physical AI and humanoid robotics applications. The guide covers everything from basic robotic platforms to advanced sensor systems, actuator technologies, and control architectures necessary for building and operating humanoid robots.

## Hardware Categories

### Robot Platforms

**Research Platforms:**
- Humanoid robots for academic and research use
- Modular designs for experimentation
- Open-source hardware options
- Simulation-compatible platforms

**Commercial Platforms:**
- Industry-ready humanoid systems
- Service robot platforms
- Educational robots
- Development kits

### Actuator Systems

**Servo Motors:**
- High-precision position control
- Integrated feedback systems
- Various torque and speed ratings
- Communication protocols (CAN, RS-485, etc.)

**Series Elastic Actuators:**
- Compliant actuation for safety
- Force control capabilities
- Human-safe interaction
- Advanced control algorithms

**Pneumatic/Hydraulic Systems:**
- High power-to-weight ratios
- Dynamic movement capabilities
- Specialized control requirements
- Maintenance considerations

### Sensor Systems

**Proprioceptive Sensors:**
- Joint encoders and position sensors
- IMU (Inertial Measurement Units)
- Force/torque sensors
- Current and voltage monitoring

**Exteroceptive Sensors:**
- Vision systems (cameras, LIDAR, depth sensors)
- Audio processing systems
- Tactile sensors and skin
- Environmental sensors

### Control Systems

**Computing Platforms:**
- Real-time computing systems
- GPU-accelerated processing
- Edge AI computing
- Distributed control architectures

**Communication Systems:**
- Real-time communication protocols
- Wireless and wired networks
- Safety-critical communication
- Multi-robot coordination

## Learning Objectives

By studying this hardware guide, students will be able to:

- Understand different humanoid robot platforms and their applications
- Select appropriate actuators for specific robotic tasks
- Integrate various sensor systems for comprehensive perception
- Design control architectures for real-time robotic operation
- Evaluate hardware trade-offs for specific applications
- Plan hardware integration and safety considerations

## Hardware Selection Criteria

### Performance Requirements

**Load Capacity:**
- Static and dynamic load specifications
- Center of mass considerations
- Structural integrity requirements
- Safety factors and margins

**Speed and Accuracy:**
- Position and velocity requirements
- Acceleration capabilities
- Precision and repeatability
- Response time constraints

**Environmental Considerations:**
- Operating temperature ranges
- Dust and moisture protection
- Shock and vibration tolerance
- Electromagnetic compatibility

### Cost and Accessibility

**Budget Considerations:**
- Initial hardware costs
- Maintenance and operational costs
- Replacement part availability
- Long-term support and updates

**Accessibility:**
- Availability of components
- Geographic distribution
- Lead times and supply chain
- Educational discounts and programs

### Development Support

**Documentation:**
- Technical specifications
- Integration guides
- API documentation
- Example code and applications

**Community Support:**
- Active developer communities
- Forum and support resources
- Third-party integrations
- Educational materials

## Safety and Compliance

### Safety Standards

**International Standards:**
- ISO 13482 for service robots
- ISO 12100 for machinery safety
- IEC 62061 for safety-related control systems
- ISO 10218 for industrial robots

**Electrical Safety:**
- Power system safety
- Emergency stop systems
- Electrical isolation requirements
- Ground fault protection

### Risk Assessment

**Mechanical Hazards:**
- Pinch points and crushing
- Cutting and shearing
- Entanglement risks
- Projectile hazards

**Electrical Hazards:**
- Electric shock risks
- Fire hazards
- Electromagnetic interference
- Battery safety

## Week Summary

This hardware guide provides the foundational knowledge needed to understand and select appropriate hardware for Physical AI and humanoid robotics applications. The guide emphasizes the importance of matching hardware capabilities to application requirements while maintaining safety and reliability standards.
